Objection Handling Cheat Sheet

Every objection is a buying signal. Here's how to handle the most common ones.

AI can't write production-quality code
It already is — for our current clients, right now. But here's the key: our agents never work alone. Every pull request is reviewed by a senior engineer before it touches your codebase. The agents handle the volume — bug fixes, feature additions, routine work. Our engineers handle quality control, complex decisions, and anything the agent flags as beyond its confidence level. We built the engine that Bullhorn acquired — we know how to ship production software.
We tried Copilot / ChatGPT and it wasn't good enough
Copilot is an autocomplete tool. ChatGPT is a chatbot. Our agents are autonomous workers. The difference is like comparing a spell-checker to a full-time writer. Our agents pick up tickets, clone repos, write code, create PRs, and iterate on feedback — without anyone sitting in front of a screen prompting them.
What about security? We can't have AI touching our codebase
All code goes through your normal PR review process — nothing gets merged without human approval. The agent runs on our managed infrastructure (or yours if you prefer). We can sign an NDA and work within your security requirements. Our agents are already trusted with production codebases for enterprise clients.
This sounds too cheap — what's the catch?
No catch. We can charge less because AI dramatically reduces our cost to deliver. Traditional agencies have 10 developers billing $200/hr each. We have AI agents doing the repetitive work and senior engineers handling the complex decisions. Our margins are healthy — we're just sharing the efficiency gains with you instead of keeping them all.
We'd rather hire a full-time developer
A junior dev costs $5-8K/month including benefits, takes months to ramp up, needs management, takes PTO, and might leave in a year. Our agent + senior engineer oversight starts at $3K/month, produces on day one, works 24/7, never quits, and gets smarter over time. And our engineers handle whatever the agent can't — complex architecture, tricky UI work, client-facing guidance. Month-to-month, cancel anytime. Try getting that flexibility with a hire.
Can we do a trial / pilot first?
Absolutely. That's actually how we prefer to start. Pick your 3-5 highest-priority tickets and we'll have our agent work through them. You'll see real PRs in your repo within the first week. No long-term commitment — if it doesn't deliver, you cancel.
We already have an agency
We're not asking you to fire them. Start with one agent alongside your current team. If our dev agent costs $4K/month and handles the work of a $12K/month developer on your agency's bill — you'll know pretty quickly which is the better deal. We often work alongside existing teams.

Sales PlaybookSales Process & Onboarding

Sales Process

The Pipeline
1

Outreach / Inbound

Lead comes in via gazellia.com contact form, cold outreach (LinkedIn, email), or referral. Qualify: Do they have a dev team or agency? What's their monthly spend? What tools do they use (Monday.com, Jira, GitHub)?

2

Discovery Call (30 min)

Understand their workflow, pain points, and current spend. Map out where an agent would fit. Use the vertical playbook for their industry. Don't pitch pricing yet — focus on the problem.

3

Custom Proposal

Send the pricing PDF (Services & Pricing doc) with a custom cover note tailored to their specific use case. Highlight the cost comparison vs. their current approach. Include a recommended agent package.

4

Pilot / Close

Offer a pilot: 3-5 tickets for the agent to work through in the first week. No contract for the pilot — just month-to-month. Once they see real PRs landing in their repo, the close happens naturally.

5

Onboarding (Week 1)

Get access to their repo (GitHub/GitLab), project board (Monday.com/Jira), and Slack. Configure the agent. First tickets processed within 24-48 hours. Weekly check-in call for the first month.

Qualification Checklist

Green Flags

  • Already using Monday.com, Jira, or Linear
  • Spending $5K+/mo on developers or agency
  • Growing backlog they can't keep up with
  • Asking about AI — curious but unsure where to start
  • Previous bad experience with offshore devs

Red Flags

  • Budget under $2K/month
  • No existing codebase (need strategy, not agents)
  • Want to "try AI" with no clear use case
  • Decision requires 6+ stakeholders / committee
  • Expecting 100% autonomous with zero oversight
